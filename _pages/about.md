I am a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Rochester, working with [Sam Norman-Haignere](https://www.urmc.rochester.edu/labs/computational-neuroscience-audition). My research asks how the human brain transforms complex acoustic signals — speech, music, and environmental sounds — into meaningful percepts, even under challenging listening conditions.

I use intracranial EEG and fMRI to study the computational architecture of human auditory cortex. My current work, funded by an NIH/NIDCD F32 postdoctoral fellowship, investigates how the brain constructs invariant representations of speech.
